on desire



They say that not wanting something is as good as having it.



But I have also heard that Wanting something bad enough is as good as having it.



It appears as though it is the lukewarm wants that lie in between these two extremes that precisely cause suffering.



While these wants are not strong enough to disturb the inertia of oneâ€™s life, they are not yet weak enough to be discarded.



The result is a state of purgatory where one spends all of their time collecting more and more baggage without arriving anywhere.



What does it mean to Want something bad enough?



One can only "Want something bad enough" once their desire is sincere.



When one's desire is sincere, they become sufficiently serious.



When one becomes sufficiently serious, they gain complete access to every possible resource at their disposal.



When one has every possible resource at their disposal, what they Want is no longer a dream ... but an inevitability.
